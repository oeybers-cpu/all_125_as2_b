‚úçÔ∏è WRITING YOUR REFLECTION

Your four-paragraph essay must address these sections:

1. Introduction and Overview

Introduce your chosen text (author, title, publication context, main topic). Briefly explain how this text connects to your personal and academic interests. State your main response to the text.

2. Personal and Cultural Connections

Connect the text's main ideas or themes to your personal background, cultural context, or lived experiences. Why do these ideas matter to you? How do they appear in or influence your own life? Explain how your identity shapes your perspective of this text.

3. Argument Analysis

Identify the author's main argument or purpose. Analyse specific writing strategies the author uses to make their point (evidence, appeals, structure, language choices). Comment on these strategies - what works, what doesn't, and why.

4. Disciplinary Reflection

Connect the text to your academic discipline or broad academic interests. Comment on how this text might contribute to broader discussions in your field/related academic disciplines.
