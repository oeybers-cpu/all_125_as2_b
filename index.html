Demo Assignment: Mental Health and Youth Wellbeing

Part A: Essay

Chosen Topic: Mental Health and Youth Wellbeing

Selected Text: World Health Organization (WHO) report, "Mental health of adolescents" (2021).

Full Reference: World Health Organization. (2021). Mental health of adolescents. https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/adolescent-mental-health

Essay:

The WHO report argues that adolescent mental health is a critical global crisis, driven by systemic factors like social media pressures, academic stress, economic inequality, and inadequate access to care. It emphasises that 1 in 7 adolescents globally experiences mental disorders, with depression and anxiety being most prevalent. The text frames this not as individual failure but as a societal issue requiring multi-level interventions‚Äîpolicy changes, community support, and destigmatisation of mental health services. Crucially, it highlights how marginalised youth (e.g., LGBTQ+ individuals, those in poverty) face compounded risks due to intersecting discrimination and resource gaps.

Academically, the report excels in synthesising global data to reveal patterns, but it leans heavily on Western-centric metrics of 'wellbeing' that may not capture cultural nuances. For instance, it prioritises clinical diagnoses over community-based healing practices common in Global South contexts. While it cites youth voices, the analysis could better integrate indigenous perspectives on mental health‚Äîsuch as communal care models in African or Indigenous cultures‚Äîwhich the text briefly acknowledges but doesn't centre. This gap limits its applicability in non-Western settings where mental health is often understood through spiritual or collective frameworks. My psychology studies have highlighted how diagnostic frameworks like the DSM-5 often pathologise culturally normative expressions of distress, failing to account for variations in symptom presentation across communities.

Personally, this resonates deeply with my psychology studies. During the pandemic, I observed peers experiencing academic burnout and social isolation‚Äîphenomena explained by the biopsychosocial model, where psychological distress intersects with biological and social factors. However, my community's cultural stigma around mental health aligns with Corrigan's (2004) framework on self-stigma, where internalised shame prevents help-seeking. While the WHO's systemic approach (e.g., school-based mental health programmes) is crucial, it overlooks culturally grounded interventions like narrative therapy, which my coursework highlights as vital for validating indigenous healing practices such as communal storytelling.

Ultimately, this text underscores that mental health is inseparable from social justice. To truly support youth, we must move beyond clinical solutions to address root causes: poverty, discrimination, and cultural erasure. My hope is that future policies will centre youth-led, culturally grounded approaches‚Äîlike peer support groups rooted in local traditions‚Äîrather than imposing universal 'fixes'. Only then can we build systems where every young person feels seen, heard, and valued.

Part B: GenAI Image and Translanguaging

Step 1: GenAI Image Creation

Initial Prompt (20+ words):
"A young Black South African girl standing at a crossroads: one path shows glowing support networks (therapist, friends, family), the other dark with isolation. Her translucent brain has cracks mended with golden light, surrounded by floating icons: a phone (social media pressure), a school book, and a heart. Style: digital painting, warm earth tones, hopeful atmosphere."

Refinement 1:
"Add soft blue light on the support path; replace brain cracks with glowing neural pathways; include traditional Zulu beadwork patterns woven into the light."

Refinement 2:
"Focus on the girl's hands gently holding a sprouting seedling; replace 'cracks' with interconnected roots symbolising ancestral resilience; background shows diverse hands reaching out from community spaces."

Final Selection & Explanation:
I chose Refinement 2. It shifts focus from "damage" to active healing‚Äîthe seedling represents growth, while "interconnected roots" embodies ancestral wisdom and collective strength. The "diverse hands" visually reinforce community care, which is central to isiZulu concepts of ubuntu. This image avoids Western individualism, centring interdependence instead of "fixing" brokenness.

Step 2: Translanguaging

L1 (isiZulu):
"Ukukhuluma kuyaphakade, kodwa ukuthiwa ngokukhetha, kuyathanda."

L2 (English Translation):
"Speaking heals, but only when done in chosen spaces, it brings love."

50-Word Explanation:
This isiZulu phrase encapsulates ubuntu‚Äîa core concept in cultural psychology where healing is relational. English translation loses the nuance: "chosen spaces" implies agency, but "kuyathanda" (it brings love) conveys communal emotional safety absent in Western clinical models. My psychology studies highlight how collectivist cultures frame mental health through interdependence, not individual pathology.

Your four-paragraph essay must address these sections:

1. Introduction and Overview

Introduce your chosen text (author, title, publication context, main topic). Briefly explain how this text connects to your personal and academic interests. State your main response to the text.

2. Personal and Cultural Connections

Connect the text's main ideas or themes to your personal background, cultural context, or lived experiences. Why do these ideas matter to you? How do they appear in or influence your own life? Explain how your identity shapes your perspective of this text.

3. Argument Analysis

Identify the author's main argument or purpose. Analyse specific writing strategies the author uses to make their point (evidence, appeals, structure, language choices). Comment on these strategies - what works, what doesn't, and why.

4. Disciplinary Reflection

Connect the text to your academic discipline or broad academic interests. Comment on how this text might contribute to broader discussions in your field/related academic disciplines.
